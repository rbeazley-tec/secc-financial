Despite a decline in guest volume since 2008, revenue per guest-night has grown 54% — reflecting improved pricing and a higher-value event mix. This rate growth has kept total annual revenue stable near $800K–$1.1M even as occupancy has declined.

Monthly occupancy pattern — 2021–2025 average
Strong (>50%) Adequate (35–50%) Lean (<35%)

July is peak season (68%), driven by summer retreats and youth programs. March and October are secondary peaks. December, April, and August are consistently lean — a pattern that creates cash flow challenges even when annual revenue totals are adequate.

Sustainability Analysis

What occupancy does SECC need to break even?

At the current billing rate of $144.69 per guest-night, SECC reaches cash operating breakeven at approximately 41% occupancy — nearly identical to 2025 actual performance. The center is operating at its breakeven threshold. Returning to pre-COVID occupancy levels would generate a meaningful surplus.

✓ SECC is operating at its breakeven threshold. The 2025 occupancy rate of 40.6% is essentially equal to the cash breakeven rate of approximately 41%. Returning to the pre-COVID average of 50% would generate approximately $205,000 in annual surplus over current cash operating expenses.
→ The projected deficit of ($212,694) reflects a revenue shortfall, not a cost crisis. Cash operating expenses are $1,036,194 (total budget less $9,500 non-cash depreciation). Closing the gap requires growing occupancy modestly above current levels — a realistic target given the historical record.
