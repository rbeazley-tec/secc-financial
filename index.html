Sustainability Analysis

What occupancy does SECC need to break even?

At the current billing rate of $144.69 per guest-night, SECC reaches cash operating breakeven at approximately 41% occupancy — nearly identical to 2025 actual performance. The center is operating at its breakeven threshold.

✓ SECC is operating at its breakeven threshold. The 2025 occupancy of 40.6% is essentially equal to the cash breakeven of ~41%. Returning to the pre-COVID average of 50% would generate approximately $205,000 in annual surplus over current cash operating expenses.
→ The projected deficit of ($212,694) reflects a revenue shortfall, not a cost crisis. Cash operating expenses are $1,036,194 (total budget less $9,500 non-cash depreciation). Growing occupancy modestly above current levels is a realistic and achievable path to balance.
